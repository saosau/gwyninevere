To Arch or to Gentoo, That Is the Question

Wed Dec 21, 2022 · 137 words

The answer is neither. Just use Debian.

I’m joking. In my opinion it’s a good idea to understand these 3 main ditributions. Simply put, let’s say we have a set of packages. firefox gimp and mpv. When I compile this I will get a binary:

gcc hello_world.c

Debian freezes its packages to a certain version, where the whole set of packages are rock solid. This grants stability and quick installation, all you do is download binary files.

What Arch does different is that maintainers always employ newest package possible. So it is less stable, but provides you that bleeding edge. Arch Linux is also a binary-based distribution.

On the other hand, to simplify, Gentoo is also a rolling, but source-based distribution. So it compiles packages from source.

And really, all 3 of them are brilliantly designed distributions.
